JUN 07 2026 · SUN · DAY 100

Day 100 of the Iran war. Iran fires ballistic missiles toward Kuwait and Bahrain overnight. Denies ceasefire violation. Iran fired ballistic missiles toward US bases in Kuwait and Bahrain on Saturday night — the latest exchange following Friday's US strikes on Iranian coastal radar infrastructure. Iran is simultaneously denouncing US attacks as violations of the ceasefire framework while continuing its own military operations. The ceasefire and the war are operating in parallel. Day 100 marks a round-number threshold the coverage will frame as a milestone — the operational reality has not changed.

Iranian nuclear negotiating team denied visas for Mexico talks. Iran's delegation to peace negotiations was denied a "large" number of visas needed to travel to the next round of talks in Mexico City. The US State Department has not commented publicly. The visa denial — whether deliberate policy or administrative failure — effectively delays the next negotiating round. Talks cannot happen if the Iranian team cannot travel. The war unpopularity factor is entering US political calculations: analysts say the war's declining support may hurt Republicans ahead of midterm elections, potentially increasing pressure on Trump to close a deal.

Bitcoin hits $59,100 — lowest since February. Bouncing to ~$61,822. Bitcoin touched $59,100 on Saturday before recovering to approximately $61,822 Sunday morning. RSI at 24 — deeply oversold. 13 of 15 moving averages in sell territory. The daily chart remains bearish. The bounce is occurring on declining volume — weaker buying conviction rather than a broad sentiment shift. Critical threshold: $63,000-64,000 close needed to challenge the bearish trend. Failure to hold $61,000 support risks another test of the $59,100 low.

US consumers rethinking spending as war inflation compounds. From unfilled gas tanks to reduced discretionary purchases, retailers are reporting visible behavioral change in US consumer spending patterns. Gas at $4.51/gallon — 51% above the pre-war baseline — is the primary driver. The inflation that began as a macro story is now a behavioral story at the consumer level. The transmission from war to gas price to spending pattern to economic slowdown is now visible in retail data.

Week ahead: CPI Tuesday. FOMC Wednesday June 18 on the horizon. The June 10 CPI release is the most important data point before the June 18 FOMC meeting. The binary is clean: hot CPI confirms hawkish pivot and hike scenario, cold CPI reopens cut narrative. Art Basel Basel opens June 10. Gemini New Moon also June 10 — lunation cycle reset.
