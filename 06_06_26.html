JUN 06 2026 · SAT · WEEKEND EDITION

Friday was the worst day for US markets since April 2025. The Nasdaq dropped 4.2%. S&P 500 fell 2.64% to 7,384. The Dow, which had hit a record high Thursday, declined 1.4%. Semiconductor stocks led the collapse — Broadcom fell 7%+ following a double-digit decline Thursday, Marvell Technology plunged 16%, Micron 13%, Intel and AMD each lost around 11%. The week that began with records ended in a sharp selloff driven by the tech-chip complex unraveling.

US struck Iranian radar sites after intercepting drones launched toward Hormuz. The US military said it struck coastal surveillance radar sites in Iran after shooting down drones that Tehran launched toward the Strait of Hormuz. Iran fired missiles and drones as "warning shots" — the stated reason being US harassment of Iranian marine traffic. The US Central Command confirmed the intercepts and strikes. Day 97 of Hormuz closure.

Lebanon: 21 killed in Israeli strikes Friday. Ceasefire fraying. A ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah reached June 1 — Israel agreed not to target Beirut's southern suburbs, Hezbollah agreed not to attack Israel — is under visible strain. Israel continued strikes across southern Lebanon Friday killing at least 21 people including a municipal council member in Sidon, two Syrian children, and a paramedic. Lebanon's president said the Lebanese people are "fed up" with war. Hezbollah has not formally broken the June 1 ceasefire but fighting has not stopped.

May jobs report: 139,000 added. Unemployment holds at 4.2%. The May NFP came in above the 80,000 expected — 139,000 jobs added. Unemployment held at 4.2%. The stronger-than-expected number paradoxically contributed to Friday's selloff by reinforcing the Fed's hawkish posture. A robust labor market gives the Fed less cover to cut — or more cover to hike if inflation persists.

Bitcoin closed the week at approximately $62,000. Weekly decline accelerating. BTC ATH was $126,198 Oct 2025. Currently at roughly half that. ETH tracking lower across the same period. Crypto ETF outflows sustained — institutional de-risking, not retail panic.

WEEK IN REVIEW · JUNE 1–6
THE CHIP COLLAPSE
The semiconductor sector unraveled across the week. Broadcom's double-digit decline Thursday accelerated into a sector-wide selloff Friday. Marvell, Micron, Intel, AMD all down double digits. The AI infrastructure spending thesis — which had held equities near records through three months of war — is now showing cracks. The question is whether this is a rotation or a repricing of the entire AI revenue timeline.
JOBS NUMBER PARADOX
139,000 jobs added in May. Better than the 80,000 feared. Markets fell anyway. The mechanism: a stronger labor market gives the Fed more cover to hold rates high or hike. In an environment where the market needs rate cuts to justify current valuations, good jobs data is bad market data. The economic-market decoupling is now complete.
IRAN — RUBIO VS REALITY
Secretary of State Rubio has asserted the war is "over." The US military struck Iranian radar sites on Friday. Iran launched drones toward Hormuz. 21 died in Israeli strikes on Lebanon. The gap between the diplomatic frame and the operational reality is the defining feature of the current phase. Each side is managing narrative and military operations simultaneously. Coverage struggles to hold both at once.
CRYPTO REGIME SHIFT
Bitcoin went from a potential safe-haven asset (climbed to $82,000 by May 11) to a confirmed risk-on asset (now below $62,000, pre-war levels fully erased). The regime shift is structural. ETF outflows are institutional. The war premium thesis for crypto has failed completely. What's left is a high-beta tech proxy in a tech selloff.
