Iran · Day 89. CENTCOM carried out new strikes overnight Thursday targeting what it described as a site posing threat to US forces and commercial traffic. CENTCOM also posted to X accusing Iran of an "egregious ceasefire violation." Both sides have now used this language against each other. At Wednesday's Cabinet meeting Trump said: "We're not satisfied with it, but we will be. Either that, or we'll have to just finish the job." On Hormuz control: Trump said the strait will be open to everyone and no one will control it — Iran has maintained management should be coordinated with Oman. On sanctions: Trump said Iran will not receive sanctions relief in exchange for surrendering its enriched uranium. No deal signed. Lebanon: Israel launched fresh strikes on Tyre Thursday. Lebanese MoH cumulative toll March 2–May 26: 3,213 killed, 9,737 wounded.

Ukraine · Day 1554. Zelensky's urgent letter to Trump on missile defense shortages confirmed. Overnight May 27 explosions near Voronezh bomber base — no confirmed attribution or casualties. Russia denied a US official a visa for a UN meeting; described US response as "egregious disrespect."

Ebola. Current figures as of May 26 (ECDC/DRC MoH): 105 confirmed cases, 10 deaths; 906 suspected cases, 223 suspected deaths. Uganda: 7 confirmed, 1 dead. No vaccine for Bundibugyo. No therapeutics. PHEIC active. Milan suspected cases ruled out May 25.

AI. KPMG deployed Claude to 276,000 employees across 138 countries (announced May 19, now widely reported). OpenAI launched $4 billion consulting subsidiary "DeployCo." Cohere acquired Aleph Alpha — combined $20 billion transatlantic sovereign AI company. Canada privacy regulator ruled OpenAI violated privacy law.
