Iran · Day 93. Talks suspended. Iran suspended indirect talks with the United States today. Tasnim (semi-official, IRGC-linked): "Given the continuation of the Israeli regime's attacks in Lebanon, and considering that Lebanon had been one of the preconditions for a ceasefire — which has now been violated on all fronts, including Lebanon — the Iranian negotiating team is suspending talks and exchanges of texts through mediators." Iran's lead negotiator and parliament speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf accused the US of breaching the ceasefire by continuing the naval blockade and failing to restrain Israel in Lebanon. The IRGC threatened to open "other fronts" in the war including the Bab el-Mandeb strait — the Red Sea chokepoint. IRGC also struck a base in Kuwait it says was used for an attack on its Sirik Island. Kuwait condemned the strikes. Oil prices rising sharply.

Lebanon context: Netanyahu ordered military to expand operations in Lebanon after occupying Beaufort Castle, calling it a "dramatic change." Trump on Truth Social: Iran "really wants to make a deal" and urged critics to "sit back and relax." Oil prices rising sharply on suspension news.

Ukraine · Day 1558. Russia total losses: ~1,364,060 as of May 31. 976 attacks on Zaporizhzhia yesterday — 2 killed, 7 injured. ISW: Russia net loss of 100 square miles of Ukrainian territory in the four weeks ending May 26 — largest monthly loss of the war.

Ebola. WHO DG Tedros arrived in Ituri province Saturday. 1,262 cases as of May 29. Uganda border closed. No updated figures in Monday morning sources.

US domestic. Federal judge blocked Trump's $1.8B "anti-weaponization" fund. Federal judge ruled Trump's Kennedy Center renaming illegal. Markets: record May, S&P 500 ninth consecutive week of gains. Dow and Russell 2000 dipping Monday on Iran suspension news.

OPEN QUESTIONS
BAB EL-MANDEB
Iran threatening to open the Bab el-Mandeb as another front — the Red Sea strait between Yemen and Djibouti. Houthis have already been attacking shipping there. If Iran directly operationalises this, it adds a second major chokepoint to Hormuz. Global shipping would face simultaneous closure of Suez approaches and Persian Gulf. The Houthi campaign has already rerouted significant container traffic around the Cape of Good Hope. This threat represents a potential step-change in supply chain disruption.
KUWAIT
IRGC struck a base in Kuwait it describes as having been used for an attack on its Sirik Island. Kuwait is a US ally with significant US military presence. Kuwait's Foreign Ministry condemned the strikes. Iran striking a Gulf state directly — not just a proxy action — is a geographic expansion of the conflict. Watch for US or Gulf Cooperation Council response.
DAHIEH / BEIRUT
IDF ordered evacuation of Dahieh — Beirut's southern suburbs, Hezbollah's urban stronghold. Netanyahu ordered the military to expand operations after occupying Beaufort Castle. Israeli ground forces have deployed five divisions in Lebanon. This is the first direct IDF warning directed at Beirut proper. Lebanese PM called it scorched-earth. The US reportedly was pressuring Israel not to target Beirut. That pressure does not appear to have held.
TRUMP OPTIMISM GAP
Trump posted on Truth Social that Iran "really wants to make a deal" on the same day Iran suspended talks. The gap between the White House public position and the operational reality — Iran suspending negotiations, IRGC threatening new fronts, Kuwait struck — is now explicit and measurable.
