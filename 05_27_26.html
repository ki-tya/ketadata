SITUATION SUMMARY · MAY 27 2026

Iran war Day 88. Trump meets Cabinet today on Iran negotiations. Monday's US self-defense strikes on Iranian missile launch sites and boats near Hormuz occurred during active Doha talks — Iranian delegation met Qatari mediators Monday, talks described as "generally positive" per Iran's semi-official Tasnim. The central unresolved issue in negotiations: Iran's ~1,000 pounds of highly enriched uranium. US position per officials: "no dust, no dollars." Iran's 10-point plan remains the basis for the framework. Ceasefire has been violated by both sides since April 8 (Pakistan-mediated).

Ukraine Day 1553. Explosions and fires reported overnight across multiple Russian regions and occupied Crimea, including near a bomber unit airbase in Voronezh. Kyiv Independent reports Zelensky has sent Trump an urgent letter warning of critical missile defense shortages. Pope Leo XIV condemned Russian strikes on Ukraine, calling for end to what he described as a "sharp intensification" of the war. Russia denied a US official a visa to attend a UN meeting — Russia foreign ministry described this as "egregious disrespect."

Ebola. ECDC figures as of May 26: DRC 105 confirmed cases (10 deaths), 906 suspected cases (223 deaths) in Ituri, North Kivu, and South Kivu. Uganda: 7 confirmed cases, 1 death. On May 25, two travelers from Uganda to Milan were hospitalised with hemorrhagic fever symptoms — laboratory testing ruled out Ebola. No vaccine for Bundibugyo species. No therapeutics. PHEIC active since May 17.

AI. Anthropic funding round confirmed closed: $30B+ at pre-money valuation above $900 billion — above OpenAI's $852B March 2026 private valuation. Co-led by Sequoia, Dragoneer, Altimeter, Greenoaks. Positions Anthropic as highest-valued private AI company. US politics: Ken Paxton defeated incumbent Sen. John Cornyn in Texas GOP Senate runoff.
