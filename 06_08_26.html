JUN 08 2026 · MON · DAY 101

Major escalation overnight — Israel strikes Iran, Iran retaliates, Houthis fire on Israel. In the early hours of Monday, Israel launched missile strikes on Iran — dozens of warplanes targeting Iranian air defenses that had been rebuilt since the earlier fighting. Explosions reported in Tehran, Isfahan, and Tabriz. Iranian airports shut down. Israel also struck a petrochemical complex in Mahshahr in southwestern Iran; workers evacuated. Iran retaliated and the Iranian military issued a statement saying its response has concluded — but warned that if hostilities continue, Iran's actions will be "much more severe and decisive." Houthis in Yemen also fired missiles at Israel, triggering sirens in the Tel Aviv area.

Trump: "Immediate ceasefire." Iran says retaliation complete. President Trump posted on social media Monday morning demanding Israel and Iran "immediately stop shooting" and said they are "looking to do an immediate ceasefire." He criticized Israel's strikes on Beirut separately and said he would urge Netanyahu to avoid retaliatory action against Iran while calling on Tehran to resume negotiations. Iran's military announcement that its response has "concluded" — combined with Trump's ceasefire demand — creates the structure of a possible de-escalation. Whether it holds depends on Israeli operational decisions in the next 24-48 hours.

WTI crude up 3.41% to $93.63. OPEC+ approves July production increase. Oil rose sharply on the overnight escalation. OPEC+ separately approved a 188,000 barrels per day increase in July production quotas — a modest counter-pressure — despite persistent supply risks from continued near-closure of Hormuz. The net effect: elevated but not spiking to the $106+ levels seen in earlier escalation events.

CPI tomorrow — the week's defining data point. Tuesday's May CPI release determines the Fed's June 18 FOMC framing. Gas at $4.51/gallon is already embedded. Services inflation has been sticky. The overnight escalation complicates the picture: if markets read today's Iran statement as a genuine de-escalation, the ceasefire-optimism trade (lower oil, weaker safe-haven, risk assets up) may emerge. If the Israeli strikes resume, the risk-off trade returns. CPI lands into whichever regime is operating by Tuesday morning.

Ebola — Kenya standoff continues. Court suspension of US quarantine facility still active. No confirmed fallback. DRC border deteriorating.

OPEN QUESTIONS
IRAN "CONCLUDED"
Iran's military statement that its response has "concluded" is the most structurally important signal of the morning. If genuine, it creates a pause that allows Trump's ceasefire demand to land. If performative — a declaration before the next round — then nothing has changed. The 24-48 hour Israeli operational decision is the variable. If Israel does not resume strikes, the Iran statement holds. If Israel strikes again, Iran's warning of "much more severe" action becomes the next frame. The statement and the warning are doing different work simultaneously.
TRUMP VS NETANYAHU
Trump publicly criticized Israel's strikes on Beirut and said he would urge Netanyahu to avoid retaliatory action against Iran. This is a meaningful public break — not the first, but the most explicit on the Iran question. Netanyahu's domestic electoral calendar creates incentives to continue. Trump's political calculus — war unpopularity, midterm pressure, the Iran deal as a legacy moment — creates incentives to stop. These are in direct conflict. The question is whether Trump applies actual pressure or performative pressure.
CPI INTO ESCALATION
Tuesday's CPI lands into an escalation event. If the ceasefire holds overnight and markets read the Iran "concluded" statement as real, the market regime going into CPI is risk-on, which makes a soft CPI a double catalyst. If the escalation continues, CPI lands into risk-off, and even a soft print may not produce a rally. The geopolitical variable has become the primary modifier of how the macro data is read. This is the regime of the entire war period — macro data matters, but only within whatever geopolitical frame is active.
OPEC+ JULY INCREASE
OPEC+ approved a 188,000 bpd increase for July despite ongoing Hormuz near-closure. The signal: the cartel is willing to increase supply even in an active conflict environment, either because they believe the conflict will resolve or because they want to maintain market share. The increase is modest relative to Hormuz's 20% of global supply — it does not offset the closure, but it signals a political preference for stable pricing over maximum leverage. Watch whether this moderates the oil spike from overnight.
