TODAY'S READ · MAY 23 2026

The Iran war is the story of 2026 and it is being processed as a background condition rather than the active emergency it is. Day 84. The Strait of Hormuz — through which 20% of global oil flows — has been effectively closed since February 28. The IEA called it the greatest energy security challenge in history. Brent hit $126. Today, Trump posted that a deal is "largely negotiated." Rubio is in Kolkata saying it will be solved "one way or the other." The next 48–72 hours may determine whether the global energy market gets its first coherent signal in three months — or whether the war expands.

The deal is not done. Iran wants to keep its enrichment programme. The US demands surrender of enriched uranium. Israel is still striking Lebanon — which Tehran insists is covered by the ceasefire and Washington insists is not. The new Supreme Leader (Khamenei's son, installed after the Feb 28 assassination) is untested and under hardliner pressure. Pakistan is mediating, which is itself a geopolitical realignment worth tracking.

Against this, three other simultaneous crises are running: Ebola with no vaccine expanding through active conflict zones in DRC; Ukraine-Russia entering an infrastructure destruction phase with no diplomatic track; Gaza-Lebanon with no political resolution. The world is in a multi-front attrition phase: military, energy, biological.

The United States is simultaneously executing structural shifts domestically — Fed chair replaced, immigration policy reversed for 2.4 million people, executive intervention into the central bank — that are being noted individually but not read as a coherent programme. They are a coherent programme.
