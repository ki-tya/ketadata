TODAY'S READ · MAY 24 2026

The Iran deal that Trump described as "largely negotiated" yesterday has immediately fractured in public. Phase 1 — Hormuz reopening — appears to be real. Phase 2 — the nuclear question — has been explicitly deferred to talks within 30–60 days, and Iran has already denied agreeing to surrender its enriched uranium stockpile. Iranian state media is contradicting Trump's framing of the deal. Israel is calling it a "nightmare." Senior Republican senators — the hawks Trump usually answers to — are blasting it as a "disaster." The deal may still happen. But what's happening is not the deal Trump described. It is a ceasefire with a promise to negotiate the hard part later.

The structural problem: Iran's new Supreme Leader is untested, under hardliner pressure, and has every incentive to take the Hormuz relief while stalling on nuclear commitments. Israel has every incentive to torpedo the deal via continued Lebanon strikes before phase 2 begins. The ADNOC CEO said this week that full oil flow won't return until Q1–Q2 2027 regardless — even if Hormuz opens today, the supply chain damage takes months to repair. The market relief, if it comes, will be a signal ahead of the substance.

Bitcoin broke below $75,000 overnight — $941M in liquidations. A prominent billionaire investor publicly dumped most of his holdings, saying crypto failed as a geopolitical hedge during the Hormuz crisis. This is a structural demand signal, not a price fluctuation. Ukraine saw its worst weekend shelling since March — 42 dead, UNHCR warehouse hit by ballistic missile. Ebola case count continues to climb toward a South Sudan spillover scenario.
