THE AI THESIS CRACK
The AI infrastructure spending narrative has held equities near records through three months of war, record inflation, and a hawkish Fed pivot. This week it showed the first structural crack. Broadcom's earnings miss cascaded through the chip complex in two days. The core question the selloff is asking: when does AI infrastructure spending become AI revenue at scale? The chips were built. The data centers were built. The enterprise workflow integration — the actual productivity gain the valuations are pricing — is lagging licenses sold by a significant margin. The market may be beginning to price the gap between infrastructure build and revenue realization.
JOBS PARADOX — THE INVERSION IS COMPLETE
139,000 jobs added. Better than feared. Markets fell. The economic-market decoupling is now fully inverted: good economic data is bad market data because it removes the Fed's cover to cut rates. In a normal environment, strong labor = strong market. In the current environment, strong labor = Fed stays hawkish = rates stay high = growth stock valuations under pressure = market falls. The inversion will persist until the inflation picture changes or the Fed explicitly signals cuts. Neither is imminent.
CRYPTO REGIME SHIFT — CONFIRMED
BTC opened the war at $65,878. Climbed to $82,000 by May 11 — the safe-haven thesis appearing to validate. Now at approximately $62,000 — below pre-war levels. The regime shift is confirmed: Bitcoin is a high-beta tech proxy, not a safe haven. The ETF outflow streak (11+ days, $3.45B+) represents institutional de-risking with conviction, not retail panic that reverses quickly. The ATH was $126,198 in October 2025. The current price is roughly half. The distance between the narrative (Bitcoin as digital gold, inflation hedge, war safe haven) and the price action is now impossible to paper over.
THE RECORD-TO-SELLOFF PATTERN
S&P 500 set a record Monday June 1. Dow hit a record Thursday June 5. Both indices ended the week significantly below those records. The pattern of records followed immediately by sharp selloffs suggests the market is in a distribution phase — institutional selling into retail optimism at highs. The 10-week winning streak narrative that dominated coverage Monday was replaced by "worst session since April 2025" coverage Friday. The speed of the reversal is itself a signal about the fragility of the rally's foundation.
HORMUZ + RADAR STRIKES — THE ESCALATION LADDER
Iran fires drones toward Hormuz. US intercepts them and strikes Iranian radar infrastructure. This is a new step on the escalation ladder — the US is now striking inside Iran in response to what Tehran described as "warning shots." Rubio says the war is "over." CENTCOM is conducting strikes inside Iran. Both are simultaneously true. The diplomatic track and the military track are operating in parallel. The question for next week is whether the radar strikes trigger Iranian retaliation that closes the Hormuz discussion permanently or whether both sides continue calibrated escalation while maintaining the diplomatic frame.
