THE SPLIT MARKET
S&P 500 and Nasdaq at record highs while Russell 2000 is down 0.89% and under 39% of US stocks are up today. The rally is carried by a small number of large-cap tech and energy stocks. Small caps are more sensitive to interest rates — they depend on floating-rate debt and benefit most from rate cuts. The PCE reading and oil inflation are making cuts less likely.
WHY OIL IS UP TODAY
Iran suspended talks this morning. The Strait of Hormuz — carrying roughly 20% of global oil supply — has been closed since February 28. Day 93. The IRGC is threatening the Bab el-Mandeb as a second front. If that becomes operational, two of the world's five major oil chokepoints would be simultaneously restricted. JPMorgan had projected $97/barrel if Hormuz reopened in June. That reopening is now deferred.
THE GOLD PARADOX
Gold is falling on a day of geopolitical escalation. The mechanism: oil spike → inflation fears → rate cut delays → rising real yields → gold falls. Gold peaked at $5,600 in January 2026 and has corrected to $4,527. The same war that drove the peak is now, through the inflation pathway, suppressing it.
BITCOIN AS TECH PROXY
Bitcoin has an 85.4% correlation with the Nasdaq-100 during oil price spikes. It is trading as a high-beta tech asset, not as a store of value. Spot ETF outflows are at a record streak — $1.2 billion position exit. The safe-haven thesis is not holding in the current macro environment.
IRAN SUSPENSION — THE PATTERN
This is the third time in three weeks a deal was described as imminent before conditions changed. Friday: Situation Room, no decision, terms toughened. Saturday: Iran given days to respond. Monday: Iran suspended entirely, citing Lebanon. Each iteration covered as a new event. The pattern itself — deal close, new conditions, cycle repeats — is not being covered as a pattern.
DIMON'S WARNING IN CONTEXT
S&P 500 has posted nine consecutive weeks of gains through Iran war, strikes, ceasefire violations, record inflation, and a failed Situation Room meeting. Dimon said Friday markets are "exuberant" and risks are underpriced. The market's answer: yes, correctly priced. His answer: no. The question is not whether it falls today — it's whether accumulated risk is reflected in assets sitting at all-time highs.
BAB EL-MANDEB — UNDERANALYZED
Bab el-Mandeb is the 18-mile-wide strait between Yemen and Djibouti — southern entrance to the Red Sea, gateway to the Suez Canal. Houthi attacks have already rerouted container traffic around the Cape of Good Hope, adding 10–14 days to Asia-Europe transit. If Iran directly operationalises it alongside Hormuz, two major chokepoints close simultaneously. The compounded supply chain effect is not currently being calculated in available market analysis.
