TODAY'S READ · MAY 25 2026

The Iran deal is the biggest market story in months — and the signal and the reality are diverging fast. WTI dropped 5% to ~$91 this morning on deal anticipation, down from $144 at the Hormuz crisis peak. But Iran has not signed anything. Tehran has explicitly denied agreeing to hand over enriched uranium. The Phase 2 nuclear talks, deferred 30–60 days, are where the entire substance of the conflict lives. Phase 1 is a ceasefire extension with Hormuz access — not a resolution. Trump says he won't rush. Markets are pricing a resolution that hasn't happened yet.

A gunman fired at the White House checkpoint Saturday evening — the second White House shooting incident in a month — killing himself and wounding a civilian. This delayed Trump's planned Iran announcement. The pattern of two security incidents in 30 days around the executive mansion is not being framed as a systemic story.

Ebola is accelerating. 900+ suspected cases in DRC, 220 deaths. The outbreak has now reached Kampala — Uganda's capital city of 3.6 million people. No vaccine exists for the Bundibugyo strain. A treatment centre was burned down by protesters last week. Community resistance combined with active conflict zones and urban spread is the highest-risk configuration. Coverage volume remains low relative to the scale.

Ukraine: Russia struck Kyiv overnight May 24, injuring 87. Today: 4 killed, 69 injured across multiple oblasts. The UK Secretary of State's RAF aircraft was hit by Russian GPS jamming — electronic warfare now reaching NATO military assets.
