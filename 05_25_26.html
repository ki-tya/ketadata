TODAY'S READ · MAY 25 2026

The Iran deal is the biggest market event in months — and the signal and the reality are diverging fast. WTI dropped 5% to ~$91 this morning on deal anticipation, down from $144 at the Hormuz crisis peak. But Iran has not signed anything. Tehran explicitly denied agreeing to hand over enriched uranium. Phase 2 nuclear talks are deferred 30–60 days — that is where the entire substance of the conflict lives. Phase 1 is a ceasefire extension with Hormuz access, not a resolution. Markets are pricing a deal that has not happened.

Saturday evening a gunman fired at the White House checkpoint — the second White House shooting incident in 30 days — killing himself and wounding a civilian, delaying Trump's planned Iran announcement. The pattern of two security incidents in a month is not being framed as a systemic story in any major outlet.

Ebola is accelerating. 900+ suspected cases in DRC, 220 deaths. The outbreak has now reached Kampala — Uganda's capital city of 3.6 million. No vaccine exists for the Bundibugyo strain. A treatment centre was burned by protesters last week. Community resistance combined with active conflict zones and urban spread is the scenario WHO describes as highest risk. Coverage remains low relative to scale.

Ukraine: Russia struck Kyiv overnight May 24, injuring 87. Today: 4 killed, 69 injured. The UK Secretary of State's RAF aircraft was hit by Russian GPS jamming — electronic warfare now confirmed against a NATO minister's plane.
