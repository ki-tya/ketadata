TODAY'S READ · MAY 26 2026

US and Israeli forces struck Iranian missile launch sites and vessels in the Strait of Hormuz overnight Monday while an Iranian delegation was en route to Doha for negotiations. CENTCOM described the strikes as self-defence against mine-laying activity. Iran's foreign ministry called them a ceasefire violation. The Pakistan-mediated ceasefire has been in place since April 8. The Iranian delegation, led by senior officials, met Qatari mediators in Doha. As of Tuesday morning the talks have not been formally suspended. Rubio confirmed the deal is being held up by disputes over wording. The IRGC issued a statement claiming it downed a US MQ-9 Reaper drone and fired on an RQ-4 and an F-35 — without specifying when these incidents occurred.

Separately, Russia has formally pre-notified the US and all foreign embassies of planned strikes on Kyiv's decision-making infrastructure. Lavrov called Rubio personally at Putin's request on Monday. Russia fired over 100 drones and two ballistic missiles at Ukraine overnight, including a hypersonic weapon described by RFE/RL as "powerful and new." Ukraine's air force confirmed the barrage. Kyiv's foreign ministry said Russia's threats contained "nothing new." Rubio said the US remains willing to mediate.

On Ebola: WHO has formally expressed concern over the outbreak's "scale and speed." Spread was undetected for approximately 19 days because initial lab tests targeted the Zaire strain, which returned negative. Bundibugyo was confirmed May 14. As of May 23: five confirmed Uganda cases including a health worker. No vaccine exists for the Bundibugyo species. No therapeutics confirmed effective.

Today is Arafat Day, the most significant day of the Hajj calendar. 1.8 million pilgrims including Iranian nationals are at the Mount of Mercy in Mecca. Iran also announced the restoration of internet access, with President Pezeshkian ordering the rollback of restrictions in place since approximately February 28.

HYPERSONIC USE UKRAINE
Russia used a hypersonic ballistic missile in the overnight barrage. RFE/RL describes it as "powerful and new." If this is the Oreshnik (first combat use after November 2024 test), it represents a qualitative weapons escalation: Oreshnik cannot be intercepted by current Ukrainian air defence systems. Attribution to a specific system has not been independently confirmed in available sources. The distinction between "hypersonic missile used in barrage" and "drone attack" is being elided in coverage.
EBOLA DETECTION ARCHITECTURE
The 19-day detection gap is the most structurally significant data point in the Ebola outbreak. Labs tested for Zaire (the more common strain), returned negative, and stopped. The surveillance system did not have a protocol for testing alternate species on negative results. This is a system design failure, not a resource failure. WHO DRC incident manager confirmed: "our surveillance system didn't work." The outbreak's eventual scale is directly attributable to this gap.
