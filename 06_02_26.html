Iran · Day 94. Talks back on track. Iran suspended talks Monday citing Israel's Lebanon operations. Within hours Trump declared on Truth Social that talks were continuing at a "rapid pace." A regional source told CNN negotiations were back on track. Trump told ABC News a deal to reopen Hormuz and extend the ceasefire is reachable "over the next week." Israel-Lebanon talks are underway at the US State Department on Tuesday. Hezbollah has agreed to a US proposal for a ceasefire in which strikes on Beirut would stop. Trump declared Israeli forces would not move on Beirut after a heated phone call with Netanyahu. Netanyahu said Israel will "continue to operate as planned in southern Lebanon." The Trump-Netanyahu call was described as heated as Trump pressed Israel to scale back Lebanon operations. Sources: CNN, CBS News, Times of Israel.

Ebola · Kenya quarantine dispute. The Trump administration decided to send Americans exposed to Ebola to a quarantine facility in Kenya — a departure from prior outbreaks which sent Americans home. A Kenyan court ordered a temporary suspension of the plan. White House officials said if more Americans contract Ebola and need medical care they would be sent to Europe rather than flown to the US. Since the PHEIC declaration mid-May: approximately 1,000 cases and hundreds of deaths. Source: NBC News.

Ukraine · Day 1559. Russia total losses ~1,365,000+. No major front-line shifts confirmed in Tuesday morning sources. ISW standing data: Russia net loss of 100 sq miles in the four weeks ending May 26 — its largest monthly loss of the war.
